Chapter 0: The Power of a Good Night's Sleep

Sleep was not something I had taken part in for a long time. I suppose having your consciousness linger after death by being supernaturally embedded in the physical remains of your body is funny like that; No slow slide into unconsciousness, no dreams, no grogginess. One moment I was being literally torn to pieces by teams of sorcerers, and the next I was beneath the light of a full moon, nothing between me and thousands of innocents just aching to have their blood spill. I could tell that time had passed thanks to the quality of the air, the sense of the land, that sort of thing. But I, strictly speaking, was not around for those thousand years.

So it's come as a bit of a shock to the system that now I have to sit around and do nothing for roughly eight hours every single day. At least when the brat is awake I can occupy myself by focusing on the sensations he's experiencing, the things going on around him, I can speak to him, whether physically or directly in his head informing him about how annoying he is.

But when he falls asleep? It's just me and a paralyzed body.

I can Enchain him in this state, of course, which I'd already done plenty of times. Unfortunately in doing so I'd inadvertently discovered that humanity had been busy developing something called 'surveillance cameras' in my time away. Luckily nobody realized it was specifically me possessing him, and the idiot just assumed he'd been sleepwalking. But with no idea where Satoru Gojo may be at any moment, and with nothing specifically that I even wanted to do, I'd just been letting the idiot keep his body for himself, at least for now.

So here I was, the greatest Curse ever known to human kind, bored out of my mind because some teenager was sleeping. I'd been keeping myself occupied at night primarily by experimenting with Cursed Energy. While being trapped in another's body was mostly an inconvenience, a bit of exploration showed that there were interesting depths to be mined from this unique situation.

Deep in meditation on my skulls I concentrated on the murky stirrings of my vessel's thoughts. I can't read them directly (unfortunately), but I can sense roughly his state of mind. The consequences of housing two souls in one body aren't fully understood by anybody, since this was the first time it's ever happened as far as I know. And I sure didn't get where I am today without being very willing to experiment with Cursed Energy. Right now he was deep in sleep--definitely dreaming. Perfect--if anything went wrong he would undoubtedly write it off as a simple nightmare.

Even though this was just a mental representation of myself, I still brought my fingers together as I called forth Malevolent Shrine. Failing to follow the physical rituals for Curse Techniques tends to have subtle but significant consequences. I smirked as I felt the power drag his mind into my sphere of influence. I also made sure to shrink it down to roughly the size of the bed--didn't need anybody stumbling into what I was up to by accident.

"Ok so Fushiguro, you want burgers, and Jennifer you said you wanted sa...lmon?.." For a moment the idiot peered around in shock at my Domain. I couldn't help a gleeful grin--this was a level of potential torment I had been totally unaware of! I raised my hand, preparing a Fillet. He'd probably be so finely chopped it would look like ground meat.

The next moment my mind fractured. I saw myself standing there, arm raised, and felt equal fear and anger rising in my stomach at the sight of a total stranger wearing my face. And yet I could still see the kid standing there and feel my hand make the gesture to attack. "Wh-OW!" I dodged, and cried out in pain at the slash on my arm the attack made. I growled in frustration at such a sloppy move--but the gash in my shoulder--it's not mine--"What'd you do with my friends!?" A hot mess of emotions filled me as I rushed at the pink-haired shrine priest.

Fists lit with Cursed Energy came far closer than they should've as I tried to fight off this... whatever this child's mind attack was.
